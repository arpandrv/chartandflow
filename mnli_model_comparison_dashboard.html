üîç Key Insights & Recommendations

üéØ Production Recommendation

roberta-large-mnli (ONNX INT8) offers the best balance of accuracy (79.5%), reasonable model size (350MB), and acceptable inference time (1.6s). It demonstrates strong performance across most intent classes and provides the most reliable classification results.

üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Speed vs Accuracy Trade-off

distilbart-mnli-12-1 provides faster inference (1.1s) with only slightly lower accuracy (77.2%) but requires 3.4x more storage space. Consider this for real-time applications where storage isn't constrained.

üåè Cultural Adaptation Challenge

All functional models show significant performance degradation on Aboriginal English (13.6-14.9% accuracy drop). This indicates a critical need for fine-tuning or domain adaptation to improve cultural inclusivity for the AIMhi-Y chatbot.

üëã Greeting Detection Problem

Greeting intent is consistently problematic across all models, with accuracy ranging from 0% to 51.2%. This suggests that greeting patterns in both dialects may not be well-represented in the original MNLI training data.

‚öôÔ∏è Implementation Issues

bert-base-uncased-mnli completely failed due to pipeline parsing errors, highlighting the importance of robust error handling and thorough testing in production NLP systems.

üìà Intent-Specific Performance

Goal-related and Question intents perform consistently well across models, while Affirmation/Negation and Greeting intents show the most variability, suggesting these may require specialized handling or additional training data.
